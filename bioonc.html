Genentech

iPad app + Interactive Wall Display.




Background

Genentech needed a way to help medical oncologists, hematologic oncologists, fellows, and researchers find specific information about the new molecules in their vigorous pipeline.


My role

  • Research
  • •
  • UX Design
  • •
  • Content Developent


01:  Research

Stakeholder interviews

I met with internal and client stakeholders to gain an understanding of the business needs, requirements, and goals. I continued to hold multiple touchpoints as the project progressed to clarify these goals as the project took shape.

Information architecture

Due to an extremely accelerated timeline, I developed the site structure in unison with the content. This method allowed the final product to be as user-friendly as possible while keeping the project on time and budget.




02:  UX

Sitemaps

As you can see, Genentech has a very robust Biooncolgy pipeline.


iPad wireframes

Working directly with my art director partner, I used the content document as a guide to hand-draw the wireframes. These were used to develop the final UI.



03:  UI

Booth experiences

Phase 2 of the project involved taking the iPad App project files and converting them into an interactive wall display. I worked closely with my art director partner and the development team to make sure there was a seamless experience between the two.
