Billah Law Firm

End-to-end responsive web design.




Background

After acquiring front-end development skills, I immediately put them to a real-world test. I found a side client in desperate need of a new website, pitched and sold my services, and then I went to work creating a product from end-to-end.

01:  Research

Stakeholder interviews

Initially, I met with the firm's principle to gain an understanding of the business needs, requirements, and goals. I continued to hold multiple touchpoints as the project progressed to clarify these goals as the project took shape.

Competitor benchmarking

After conducting Stakeholder Interviews, I evaluated the competitive landscape to identify opportunities for innovation and differentiation. At which point, I discovered an environment bloated with content and near parity among the competition.

Information architecture

I organized the site structure in unison with the content flow to streamline the process. By doing so, this allowed us to make the final product as user-friendly as possible and complete the project within the parameters of a tight timeline.

Ideation workshops

At multiple touchpoints, I conducted in-person and virtual workshops with the client and key user groups to ensure that ideation, design, and content were genuinely connecting with the users and staying as user-centric as possible.




02:  UX

Wireframes

The Stakeholder Interviews revealed that the website needed to be simple, distinct, and straightforward. Starting with wireframes, we quickly landed on single page experience. The single-page allowed us to deliver the content in small, easily digestible chunks. Also, the location of the firm being in NYC needed to be paramount.

Motion UI elements

With a simple one page approach, I felt the site needed a few motion interactions to enhance the experience and usability. We also wanted to add a bit of delight for the user, and also communicate the thoughtful quality of the firm.


03:  UI

Final design

To help capture the seemingly opposite characteristics of the firm, that being a boutique shop with an extensive reach. And one that combines an agile approach with an established track record, I settled on a hi-contrast black and white background layered with fresh pops of muted purple. This combination exudes an aura of established credibility while directly speaking to the modern approach the firm takes with its clients.

Color palette

I approached the color selection with elegance and usability in mind. The design needed to balance the modern approach of the firm with its well-established nature. The use of hi-def black and white photography and pops of purple balanced these perfectly. The pops of color also clearly defined the critical areas of content and interface.

Typography

Similar to the color palette, I needed to capture the dual characteristics of the firm. To achieve that balance, I picked a more traditional font for Headlines and Labels to capture the firm's well-established nature and paired it with a modern sans serif to highlight their agile approach.


04:  DEV

Grid

With responsive design in mind, I choose to use a 12 column grid set to 1280px with a gutter on 30px (15x2). Breakpoints at 1024 for tablet landscape, 768px for tablet, and 480px for mobile devices.

Code

I started with Zurb Foundation 6. This framework allowed me to build each section by section rapidly. After the initial build, I needed to customize further, so I layered original CSS on top and added more complex animated interactions using Javascript and jQuery.
